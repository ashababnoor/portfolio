A Memorable Day

Today the memory I'll be sharing with you guys is a very heavy-hearted one for me. So, the year is 2011. November 29th. It was a tuesday. It was the day before the last exam of PECE. Since most of you are from bangla medium schools, you know what I'm talking about. I still vividly remember the day. We had our social science exam on that day. The next day was my religion exam. I had just came out of the hall. I was a bit upset because I was unsure about two questions that carried one mark each. It was a big deal back then. My father was slightly dissapointed hearing that. So, we all went home. I kept panicking and started crying. At that time my father received a call and had to leave to meet someone. Approximately an hour later my mother called my father to know about his where-abouts. He said he's not feeling well and that he would come back very shortly. Let me point out that my father was a patient of high blood pressure at that time. Around 10 minutes later, he comes home. He rang the door bell but when we opened the door he was about to collapse. We barely managed to get him inside. We sat him down on the sofa, he had lost control of his arm and legs. We quickly called a local doctor and when he arrived the doctor said his pressure is too high and he needs to be hospitalized immediately. So we rushed him to the hospital. He was admitted to the emergency ward and we had a ct scan done of him. We came to know that there was around 50% hemorrhage on his brain and the whole right side of his body was paralyzed. His speech was also impared. The doctors operated on my father's brain later that night. The operation was successful and he was on his way to recovery but he needed to be kept under observation so he was staying at the hospital for about a month. While still being there suddenly one very unfortunate day he had a drug reaction from the prescribed medicine which the hospital was giving him. Apparently the chances of this reaction 1 in 10 million. His condition deteriorated exponentially. The doctors said he wouldn't survive. He was kept in the icu unit for the next 2 months. But by the grace of Allah Subhan’a wa ta'ala he slowly recovered. He is alive now but unfortunately, he has been for the last 8 years and still is paralyzed. But we are lucky that he survived. That would be all. Thank you.
